Chapter 1: The Arrival

The rain fell in sheets, obscuring the winding road that led to Blackwood village. Detective Alex Reed gripped the steering wheel tighter, the wipers struggling to keep pace with the downpour. He'd taken this assignment hoping for a quiet break from city life, but the tension in Police Chief Miller's voice during their phone call suggested otherwise.

"Three disappearances in as many months," Miller had said. "All near the forest. The locals are spooked, and frankly, so am I."

As Alex rounded the final bend, the village came into view—a cluster of old stone buildings nestled against an impossibly dense wall of trees. The Silent Forest, they called it. The name seemed fitting. Even from this distance, the woods had a presence, a watchful quality that made the back of Alex's neck prickle.

He pulled up outside the police station, a modest building with a single flickering light above the door. Chief Miller was waiting, his face etched with worry lines that seemed too deep for a man his age.

"Detective Reed," Miller said, extending a hand. "Thank you for coming. I know this isn't the kind of case you're used to."

Alex shook the offered hand. "Missing persons are always serious, Chief. Tell me everything."

Miller led him inside, to a small office where case files covered every available surface. "The first was a hiker, experienced, knew these woods well. Then a teenager from the village, last seen heading toward the forest edge. Most recently, one of our own—a young officer who went to investigate reports of strange lights."

"No bodies? No signs of struggle?" Alex asked, already scanning the files.

"Nothing. It's like the forest swallowed them whole." Miller's voice dropped to a near whisper. "Some of the older villagers say it's the Guardian—an ancient spirit that protects the woods. They say it takes those who disrespect the forest."

Alex suppressed a sigh. Superstition. It always complicated investigations. But as his eyes fell on a photograph of the missing officer—a young woman with bright, determined eyes—he felt a familiar resolve settle in his gut. Superstition or not, people were missing, and he would find out why.
